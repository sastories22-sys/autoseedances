If you've ever sat in front of Seedance AI clicking "generate" one prompt at a time, you already know the problem: producing a real library of cinematic AI video takes hours of mechanical clicking. **Seedance AI automation** changes that. With the right workflow, you can queue hundreds of prompts, walk away, and come back to a folder full of finished clips. This guide shows exactly how to do that using **Auto Seedance**, a free Chrome extension purpose-built for **bulk AI video generation**.

## Why automate Seedance AI prompts at all?

Manual generation doesn't scale. A single Seedance AI clip takes 30–60 seconds to render, plus the time to type the prompt, pick settings, wait, and download. Multiply by 100 prompts and you're looking at three or four hours of focused screen time — for a workflow a computer can run on its own.

Automating Seedance prompts unlocks three things creators care about:

- **Throughput.** Hundreds of clips overnight instead of dozens per session.
- **Consistency.** Every prompt is submitted with the same settings, so output style stays uniform across a batch.
- **Time recovery.** You go back to writing prompts, editing, or shooting — the parts of the workflow software can't do.

## What is Auto Seedance?

Auto Seedance is a free Chrome extension that turns Seedance AI and Dreamina into a queue-driven, **set-and-forget workflow automation** system. You paste a list of prompts, hit start, and the extension drives the page for you: filling the input, clicking generate, waiting for completion, and downloading each finished video to your device.

It runs **inside your own browser session**, so it uses your account, your settings, and your normal access — there is no API key, no server, no separate app to install.

## Step 1 — Install the Auto Seedance Chrome extension

Head to the [Auto Seedance homepage](/) and click **Install Extension**. The extension installs into Chrome (and any Chromium browser — Edge, Brave, Arc, Opera) in one click. There is no signup, no payment, and no account required to start using it.

Once installed, pin the icon to your toolbar so you can open the Auto Seedance panel from any tab.

## Step 2 — Open Seedance AI in your browser

Sign in to Seedance AI the way you normally do. Auto Seedance does not replace the Seedance interface — it works on top of it. Whatever plan, settings, or quota you have on Seedance is exactly what your queued jobs will use.

If you want to automate Dreamina instead (or in addition), open Dreamina in another tab. Auto Seedance supports both platforms.

## Step 3 — Prepare your prompt list

This is the part that decides whether your batch is mediocre or great. A few quick rules for prompts that survive **bulk AI video generation**:

### Keep each prompt self-contained

The extension submits prompts independently. Don't rely on context from a previous prompt. Each one should describe the full scene, subject, action, and style.

### Use a consistent prompt skeleton

Pick a skeleton like `[Subject] [doing action] in [environment], [camera], [lighting], [style]` and reuse it across the whole batch. Consistent structure produces consistent output.

### Lean on cinematic language

Seedance AI responds well to film vocabulary — *dolly in*, *tracking shot*, *low angle*, *golden hour*, *volumetric light*. These words are doing a lot of work; they're trained into the model.

### Save the list as plain text

One prompt per line, no numbering, no quotes. Paste it directly into the Auto Seedance panel.

## Step 4 — Queue and start the batch

Open the Auto Seedance extension panel on the Seedance AI tab. Paste your prompt list. Confirm your generation settings (aspect ratio, duration, model) are what you want — they will be reused for every prompt in the queue.

Click **Start**. From this point on, the extension takes over:

1. Submits the first prompt.
2. Waits for Seedance AI to finish rendering.
3. Downloads the finished clip to your device.
4. Moves to the next prompt and repeats — until the queue is empty.

You can close the panel, open new tabs, or leave the machine running overnight. Auto Seedance keeps the Seedance AI tab active and works through the queue in order.

## Step 5 — Review your downloaded library

Every clip is named after its prompt (or numbered, depending on settings) and saved to your normal Chrome downloads folder. From there you can review, cull the misses, and pull the winners into your editor.

A typical batch of 100 Seedance AI prompts takes about an hour of unattended runtime — versus three to four hours of manual clicking.

## Tips for getting more out of automated Seedance AI runs

### Batch variations of the same idea

The biggest win from automation is the freedom to run *variations*. Instead of one careful prompt, write five versions of the same shot — different lighting, different camera angle, different style. Queue all five. Keep the best.

### Mix Seedance AI and Dreamina

Auto Seedance handles **Dreamina automation** too. A common workflow is to generate base images on Dreamina, then run image-to-video prompts on Seedance AI — both queued through the same extension.

### Use prompt templates from your own winners

Once you find a prompt structure that consistently works, save it as a template. Your batches become assembly lines: paste in subjects, run the queue, repeat.

### Schedule overnight runs

Long batches finish best overnight. Queue 200 prompts before bed; wake up to a fresh library.
